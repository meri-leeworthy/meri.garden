| Cubitt v Monash Health (Human Rights) [[2024] VCAT 656](https://www.austlii.edu.au/cgi-bin/viewdoc/au/cases/vic/VCAT/2024/656.html) | VCAT – member Smith |     | VCAT finds that the Respondent directly discriminated against the Applicant on the grounds of industrial activity, contrary to section 18(a) Equal Opportunity Act 2010 (Vic) (EOA), when between 23 May 2021 and 20 June 2021 it delayed in supplying a reference to support a variance from part-time permanent work to casual work in the Monash Health Bureau.<br><br>2 VCAT finds that the Respondent directly discriminated against the Applicant on the grounds of industrial activity, contrary to section 16(c) EOA, when it refused her application to the Monash Health Bureau.<br><br>…<br><br>5 By reason of order 1 above, the Respondent will pay to the Applicant $5000.00 in compensation for non-economic loss.<br><br>6 By reason of order 2 above, the Respondent will pay to the Applicant $15,000.00 in compensation for non-economic loss and $20,000.00 for economic loss.<br><br>  <br><br>  <br><br>Discrimination based on industrial activity <br><br>- $20,000 economic loss <br>- $20,000 general damages |
